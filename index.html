A focused, filterable view of AI/ML-enabled device approvals since 2016. Use the controls to explore growth, diversification, and the arrival of new companies and product codes.

-> Source: FDA AI/ML-Enabled Medical Devices list

What this dashboard shows

Each row is an FDA-posted AI/ML-enabled device entry with a final decision date, panel, and product code. The view focuses on 2016 onward to highlight the recent acceleration.

  • Filters update every chart and the device list, so you can isolate panels, years, or specific companies.
  • Approvals per year reflect the volume of FDA list entries, not unique device families.
  • New entrants track first-time appearances of companies and product codes within this dataset.
  • Time-to-decision uses openFDA 510(k) and PMA records to estimate days from submission to decision.
  • Submission numbers link directly to the FDA record for each device.

Methodology and notes

  • Data source: FDA AI/ML-Enabled Medical Devices list (downloaded as XML and parsed into structured fields).
  • The dashboard focuses on approvals from 2016 onward to emphasize the recent acceleration in AI-enabled devices.
  • Counts reflect the FDA list entries as posted; this view does not normalize for device families or company reorganizations.
  • "New entrants" counts capture the first appearance year of a company or product code within the dataset.
  • Time-to-decision uses openFDA 510(k) and PMA records; De Novo submissions are excluded because openFDA does not provide De Novo data.
  • Filtering by panel/search updates all charts except the yearly bar chart range highlight.
  • Future enrichment ideas: add clearance type, device class, modality/task taxonomy, and geographic metadata.

Glossary

  • Approval (entry): A single row from the FDA AI/ML list with a final decision date.
  • Panel: The FDA medical specialty review panel associated with the device.
  • Product code: FDA classification code that describes the device type.
  • Submission number: The FDA identifier (K, P, or DEN prefix) linking to the official record.
  • New entrant: A company or product code appearing for the first time in this dataset.
  • Time to decision: Days between FDA received date and decision date (openFDA 510(k)/PMA only).
  • Device and company: Names as listed on the FDA source page.
